  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  One-Sentence Summary: The Fabricator Robot is a ground-anchored,
  earth-pressing machine that ingests the Excavator's coarse-qualified
  soil feed, conditions it minimally, compresses it to a fixed geometry
  using the Earth itself as the compression anvil, and tunes fill volume
  dynamically via Verification feedback until the calibration loop
  converges on a stable, spec-conforming block.

1. Purpose

The Fabricator Robot is the machine that turns raw earth feed into
usable structural blocks.

It sits between the Excavator (which liberates and coarse-qualifies
soil) and Verification (which certifies the finished block). Its job is
purely mechanical and process-oriented.

It is not trying to be:

- A soil scientist

- A quality inspector

- A structural engineer

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
    Ingest coarse earth feed. Condition minimally. Compress to correct
                    geometry. Hand off to Verification.

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

2. Where the Fabricator Starts

Unlike other robots in the system, the Fabricator's constraint space was
already 70% resolved by the time it was defined — because every other
robot's boundary had already been drawn.

The known constraints entering design:

- Input is fixed: Excavator hands it a coarse-qualified soil feed via
  surface sorting container. Fabricator does not dig.

- Output is fixed: Verification is waiting to certify the finished block
  against the House Brain's recipe. Fabricator does not self-diagnose
  quality.

- Material is fixed: In-situ resource utilization. The ground the house
  sits on is the primary structural material. Zero supply chain.

The only open problem was the mechanics of turning that earth into a
spec-conforming block — cheaply, reliably, at field scale.

3. End-to-End Simple Analogy

Think of baking cookies with a cookie-cutter press.

- The House Brain is the recipe: this shape, this thickness, this
  density.

- The Excavator is the flour mill: it delivers coarse-qualified raw
  material.

- The Fabricator is the person pressing each cookie to the exact same
  size and handing it to the oven.

- Verification is the oven and the quality checker: it confirms the
  cookie came out right.

The hard part is not the pressing itself. The hard part is that the
flour changes every batch — sometimes dry and powdery, sometimes clumpy
and humid. If you press to a fixed pressure, the cookies come out
different sizes. If you press to a fixed size, the thin batches come out
crumbly.

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  The transmutation: Always press to the fixed size. Then adjust how much
  flour goes into the mold based on what Verification says about the last
  cookie. The size never changes. The fill volume does.

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

4. Final C2A Logic

The final doctrine of Fabricator:

- Do not try to generate compression force from robot mass alone

- Anchor into the Earth and borrow its mass as the anvil

- Always press to a fixed geometry — Assembly depends on dimensional
  consistency

- Adjust fill volume, not target size, to compensate for variable soil
  density

- Confirm anchor seating before every compression cycle — not assumed

- Detect incoming moisture before pressing — apply minimum conditioning
  only

- Let Verification close the quality loop — Fabricator learns from
  feedback, not from onboard sensors

- Convergence limit protects against bad soil patches that can never
  calibrate

- First 3–5 blocks of any new soil patch are calibration blocks —
  expected and planned for

- Feed quality data back to Excavator so its gate improves over time

5. Constraints and Your Transmutations

+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Constraint 1 — The Hydraulic Leviathan Problem                        |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Problem: To make a Compressed Earth Block that won't dissolve in the  |
| rain, you must squeeze dirt with 1,000–3,000 PSI. Traditional CEB     |
| machines solve this by being massive, heavy, diesel-powered hydraulic |
| presses that weigh several tons. A lightweight cheap robot that tries |
| to push down on a mold with 3,000 PSI of force will just lift itself  |
| off the ground or tear its own chassis apart.                         |
|                                                                       |
| Simple analogy: Trying to crush a soda can with your hands while it   |
| floats in the air — you won't crush it, you'll just push yourself     |
| upward. But if the can sits on a solid table, you can press down and  |
| crush it. The table resists for you.                                  |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Transmutation: The Earth Anvil — Borrow the Ground's Mass             |
|                                                                       |
| Why not use the ground itself? Instead of robot weight resisting the  |
| press force, the ground resists the press force. Drive ground anchors |
| in, lock the robot to the Earth, then compress between the anchor and |
| the press plate. The Earth is already infinitely heavy.               |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Result:                                                               |
|                                                                       |
| - Robot drives ground screws or expanding friction anchors before any |
|   compression cycle                                                   |
|                                                                       |
| - Compression force path: anchor → Earth → press plate — not through  |
|   the robot chassis                                                   |
|                                                                       |
| - Robot mass no longer limits maximum achievable press force          |
|                                                                       |
| - Cheap, high-torque ball screw or toggle joint replaces multi-ton    |
|   hydraulic press frame                                               |
|                                                                       |
| - Same principle the Survey Robot uses: make the contact zone do the  |
|   work, not the whole machine                                         |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+

+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Constraint 2 — The Wet Flour Problem                                  |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Problem: The Assembly Robot needs every block geometrically perfect   |
| to place it blindly using motor impedance. Verification needs every   |
| block at the correct density. Pressing to a fixed pressure produces   |
| consistent density but inconsistent size. Pressing to a fixed size    |
| produces consistent geometry but inconsistent density. The Fabricator |
| is a cheap robot with no onboard soil lab.                            |
|                                                                       |
| Simple analogy: If the flour is dry and powdery one day, and clumpy   |
| and humid the next, following the exact same recipe produces cookies  |
| of totally different sizes and textures.                              |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Transmutation: Dynamic Fill Volume — Fix the Size, Tune the Fill      |
|                                                                       |
| If we can't change the characteristics of the flour we can manipulate |
| its shape. We will use a reference point — the first block is what is |
| used. Every block made after must be within a threshold. The fill     |
| volume adjusts, not the target size. Verification communicates this   |
| back to Fabricator and by then Verification has enough data to guide  |
| it reliably.                                                          |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Result:                                                               |
|                                                                       |
| - Target geometry is always fixed — Assembly's dimensional            |
|   requirement is the hard constraint                                  |
|                                                                       |
| - Fill volume is the variable — more soil crammed into the same mold  |
|   space = higher density                                              |
|                                                                       |
| - If Verification reports block too light: increase fill volume for   |
|   next cycle                                                          |
|                                                                       |
| - If Verification reports block too dense or overloaded: reduce fill  |
|   volume                                                              |
|                                                                       |
| - First 3–5 blocks of each new soil patch are calibration blocks —    |
|   expected, recycled, not failures                                    |
|                                                                       |
| - System accepts calibration waste as the cost of soil variability —  |
|   explicitly planned, not improvised                                  |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+

6. Refinements

Five gaps identified after blueprint review. Each adds a safety
boundary, a sensing layer, a handling protocol, or a cross-system data
path the original two-constraint design left open.

+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Refinement 1 — The Calibration Spiral Problem                         |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Gap identified: The blueprint establishes that the first 3–5 blocks   |
| of any new soil patch are calibration blocks — they get rejected and  |
| recycled. But there is no convergence limit. A soil patch that keeps  |
| producing out-of-spec blocks after 10 iterations is not a calibration |
| problem — it is a soil problem. Without a limit, a bad soil batch can |
| consume the entire feed stream in an infinite calibration loop while  |
| the House Brain waits.                                                |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Refinement:                                                           |
|                                                                       |
| The calibration loop needs a hard convergence limit. If the dynamic   |
| fill volume adjustment has not produced an in-spec block within N     |
| cycles, the Fabricator stops, flags the soil patch to the House       |
| Brain, and requests either a new feed from a different Excavator zone |
| or a soil conditioning intervention. The convergence limit is not a   |
| failure state — it is the Fabricator correctly identifying that       |
| Verification and fill volume adjustment alone cannot fix what is      |
| wrong with this soil.                                                 |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Result:                                                               |
|                                                                       |
| - Hard convergence limit added: N calibration cycles maximum before   |
|   soil-flag escalation                                                |
|                                                                       |
| - If limit exceeded: Fabricator stops, flags to House Brain with      |
|   calibration history                                                 |
|                                                                       |
| - House Brain decides: request new Excavator zone, request            |
|   conditioning, or reclassify zone                                    |
|                                                                       |
| - Prevents infinite calibration spiral consuming the entire feed      |
|   stream                                                              |
|                                                                       |
| - Calibration cycle count added to Fabricator telemetry as a live     |
|   signal                                                              |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+

+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Refinement 2 — The Anchor Reliability Problem                         |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Gap identified: The Earth Anvil transmutation is correct — ground     |
| anchors let the Fabricator borrow the Earth's mass. But the blueprint |
| assumes the anchor seats properly on every cycle. Loose topsoil, wet  |
| ground near the excavation edge, or previously disturbed ground can   |
| all prevent the anchor from achieving the friction required to resist |
| compression force. If the anchor slips mid-cycle, the compression     |
| force transmits through the chassis instead, and block geometry is    |
| corrupted mid-press.                                                  |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Refinement:                                                           |
|                                                                       |
| Anchor seating quality must be confirmed before the compression cycle |
| begins — not assumed. The anchor drive motor's current signature at   |
| full depth is the confirmation signal. Low resistance at full depth   |
| means the anchor is spinning free in loose soil, not gripping. If     |
| current draw falls below the seating threshold, the Fabricator        |
| relocates the anchor point by a defined offset and retries. If it     |
| still cannot achieve seating confidence, it flags ground condition to |
| House Brain. Same logic the Survey Robot applies to sleeve seating    |
| confidence — the contact zone confirms its own grip before force is   |
| applied.                                                              |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Result:                                                               |
|                                                                       |
| - Anchor seating confirmation added as mandatory pre-compression step |
|                                                                       |
| - Motor current signature at full anchor depth is the seating quality |
|   signal                                                              |
|                                                                       |
| - Below-threshold current = free-spinning anchor = relocation         |
|   triggered automatically                                             |
|                                                                       |
| - If anchor cannot seat after relocation attempts: ground condition   |
|   flagged to House Brain                                              |
|                                                                       |
| - New field: anchor_seat_confidence score emitted with every          |
|   compression record                                                  |
|                                                                       |
| - Compression cycle blocked until anchor_seat_confidence exceeds      |
|   minimum threshold                                                   |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+

+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Refinement 3 — The Soil Conditioning Gap                              |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Gap identified: The Gemini session identifies 'add liquid if dry, add |
| heat if wet' as a named transmutation — but the blueprint never       |
| specifies how or when conditioning is applied, or what the trigger    |
| threshold is. Dynamic fill volume can compensate for density          |
| variation within a band. But extremely dry soil will not bind under   |
| compression regardless of fill volume. Extremely wet soil will not    |
| hold geometry after ejection. Volume adjustment alone cannot fix      |
| either extreme.                                                       |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Refinement:                                                           |
|                                                                       |
| A simple inline moisture probe on the feed intake provides a          |
| two-condition gate before fill begins. If incoming moisture is below  |
| the lower threshold, a small onboard conditioning reservoir adds a    |
| measured water dose to the mold before fill. If above the upper       |
| threshold, compression speed is reduced and a brief drying pass —     |
| heat coil or forced air — is applied before pressing. This is not a   |
| soil lab. It is a minimum conditioning gate: too dry adds water, too  |
| wet slows and dries. The Survey Robot's moisture data for the source  |
| zone should also pre-warn the Fabricator before a problematic batch   |
| arrives.                                                              |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Result:                                                               |
|                                                                       |
| - Inline moisture probe added at feed intake — single measurement per |
|   fill cycle                                                          |
|                                                                       |
| - Two-condition gate: below lower threshold = add water dose, above   |
|   upper threshold = drying pass                                       |
|                                                                       |
| - Conditioning is minimal and binary — not a soil lab, just a         |
|   boundary enforcer                                                   |
|                                                                       |
| - Survey zone moisture score consumed as a pre-batch warning signal   |
|   before problematic soil arrives                                     |
|                                                                       |
| - New fields: incoming_moisture_pct, conditioning_applied             |
|   (none/water/dry) emitted per cycle                                  |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+

+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Refinement 4 — The Block Ejection and Handling Problem                |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Gap identified: The blueprint defines compression well but says       |
| nothing about what happens after the press releases. A                |
| freshly-pressed compressed earth block at ejection is at peak         |
| vulnerability — binder not cured, block warm, corners fragile. Any    |
| lateral force during immediate transfer can chip edges or crack the   |
| block face. If the Fabricator hands a fresh block directly to         |
| Logistics or Assembly without any dwell, handling damage before       |
| Verification even sees it corrupts the quality pipeline.              |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Refinement:                                                           |
|                                                                       |
| After press release, the block is held in a support cradle for a      |
| defined ejection dwell time before handoff. This is not full cure —   |
| it is the minimum dwell for the block to achieve handling strength.   |
| The dwell time is a parameter Verification learns over time: if       |
| blocks arrive at Verification with fresh-handling damage, dwell is    |
| insufficient. Verification feeds the recommended dwell time back to   |
| the Fabricator as a tunable parameter, exactly as it feeds fill       |
| volume. New field: ejection_dwell_seconds as a Verification-tuned     |
| parameter.                                                            |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Result:                                                               |
|                                                                       |
| - Controlled ejection cradle added — block held in support position   |
|   after press release                                                 |
|                                                                       |
| - Ejection dwell time: minimum hold before handoff to                 |
|   Logistics/Assembly                                                  |
|                                                                       |
| - Dwell time is a Verification-tuned parameter — not a fixed constant |
|                                                                       |
| - If Verification sees handling damage on arrival, it increases       |
|   recommended dwell                                                   |
|                                                                       |
| - New field: ejection_dwell_seconds emitted per fabrication record    |
|                                                                       |
| - New Verification → Fabricator feedback field:                       |
|   recommended_dwell_seconds                                           |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+

+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Cross-System Refinement — Fabricator Feeds Excavator Feed Quality —   |
| Cross-System Data Path                                                |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Gap identified: The Excavator delivers a coarse-qualified feed stream |
| and considers its job done. But the Fabricator is the first system    |
| that processes that feed at fine resolution — it knows calibration    |
| cycles to convergence, fill volume adjustment required, moisture      |
| conditioning applied, and reject rate per batch. All of this is       |
| ground-truth quality data about the Excavator's output that the       |
| Excavator never receives. The Excavator's binary gate is currently    |
| tuned blind.                                                          |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Refinement:                                                           |
|                                                                       |
| After each soil batch processed, the Fabricator emits a feed-quality  |
| record to the Excavator and House Brain. This record includes         |
| calibration cycles to convergence, fill volume delta from nominal,    |
| moisture conditioning applied, and batch reject rate. The Excavator   |
| uses this to tune its binary gate aperture. If the Fabricator         |
| consistently reports high calibration cycles and heavy conditioning   |
| from a particular zone, that zone's gate threshold is too permissive  |
| — letting through borderline material that should have been rejected. |
| Over builds, the Fabricator's processing difficulty becomes the       |
| Excavator's calibration signal, tightening the whole pipeline without |
| any additional sensors.                                               |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------+
| Result:                                                               |
|                                                                       |
| - New output channel: fabricator_feed_quality_record emitted to       |
|   Excavator and House Brain after each batch                          |
|                                                                       |
| - Record includes: calibration_cycles_to_convergence,                 |
|   fill_volume_delta, conditioning_applied, batch_reject_rate,         |
|   source_zone_id                                                      |
|                                                                       |
| - Excavator uses feed quality history to tune binary gate aperture    |
|   per zone                                                            |
|                                                                       |
| - High Fabricator difficulty from zone X → Excavator tightens gate    |
|   threshold for zone X                                                |
|                                                                       |
| - Pipeline improves across builds with no additional sensors added to |
|   either robot                                                        |
|                                                                       |
| - Excavator blueprint updated: new input channel from Fabricator feed |
|   quality records                                                     |

7. Core Mission

The Fabricator Robot must:

1.  Receive coarse-qualified soil feed from the surface sorting
    container

2.  Read incoming moisture and apply minimum conditioning if outside
    threshold

3.  Drive ground anchors and confirm seating before every compression
    cycle

4.  Fill mold to current fill-volume target

5.  Compress to fixed target geometry using Earth-anchored press force

6.  Hold block in ejection cradle for Verification-tuned dwell time

7.  Hand off completed block to Verification / Logistics

8.  Receive fill volume and dwell time feedback from Verification

9.  Escalate to House Brain if calibration loop exceeds convergence
    limit

10. Emit feed quality records to Excavator after each batch

11. Emit full fabrication telemetry to House Brain

8. Functional Role

Fabricator turns:

- Coarse-qualified earth feed

- Inline moisture reading

- House Brain recipe (target geometry and density class)

- Verification feedback (fill volume and dwell corrections)

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
   ...into dimensionally consistent, density-verified structural blocks
                            ready for Assembly.

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

It is the conversion point between raw earth and structural material.

10. Mechanical Architecture

10.1 Baseline Physical Form

Recommended baseline:

- Wheeled or tracked mobile base

- Ground anchor deployment system — screw or expanding friction type

- Mold / press cavity with fixed target geometry

- Compression actuator — ball screw, toggle joint, or equivalent
  high-force low-mass mechanism

- Feed hopper intake from surface container

- Inline moisture probe at feed intake

- Minimal conditioning system — small water reservoir + optional
  heat/air element

- Ejection cradle with dwell timer

- Anchor motor with current sensing

This is not a hydraulic press on a truck. It is a field-anchored
compression cell that borrows the Earth's mass.

10.2 Ground Anchor System

Purpose: resist compression reaction force without requiring robot mass.

- Anchor deployed into undisturbed ground before each compression cycle

- Motor current at full depth is the seating quality signal

- Below-threshold current: anchor relocates by defined offset and
  retries

- Failed seating after retries: flag to House Brain, do not compress

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  Key insight: The robot is not pushing against its own weight. It is
  pulling itself between the anchor and the press plate. The Earth is the
  anvil. The Earth is also the strap.

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

10.3 Compression Mechanism

- Fixed mold geometry — target dimensions do not change

- Fill volume is the variable parameter, not press depth

- Compression force path: ground anchor → compression actuator → press
  plate → mold → Earth

- No force transmitted through robot chassis

10.4 Moisture Conditioning System

Two-condition gate — not a soil lab:

- Below lower moisture threshold: water dose added to mold before fill

- Above upper moisture threshold: drying pass (heat or forced air)
  before press

- Within range: no conditioning — proceed directly

- Water reservoir sized for a defined number of conditioning cycles
  before refill

10.5 Ejection Cradle

- Block held in support cradle after press release

- Dwell timer controlled by Verification feedback parameter

- No lateral force on block during dwell

- Handoff to Logistics/Assembly only after dwell complete

12. Software Architecture

12.1 Main Modules

- Survey moisture pre-warning consumer

- Feed intake moisture checker and conditioning gate

- Anchor deployment and seating confirmation module

- Fill volume controller — current target maintained and updated from
  Verification

- Compression cycle controller

- Ejection cradle and dwell timer

- Calibration loop manager — convergence counter and limit enforcer

- Verification feedback receiver — fill volume and dwell corrections

- Feed quality record emitter — to Excavator after each batch

- Telemetry emitter — to House Brain

12.2 Fabricator Doctrine

Stage 0 — Pre-Batch Warning

Consume Survey zone moisture score for incoming feed. If above slurry
threshold, pre-arm the conditioning gate and notify House Brain before
the first fill cycle.

Stage 1 — Moisture Check and Conditioning

Read inline moisture at feed intake. Apply conditioning if outside
bounds: water dose if too dry, drying pass if too wet. Record
conditioning_applied for telemetry.

Stage 2 — Anchor and Confirm

Deploy ground anchor. Read motor current at full depth. Confirm seating
confidence exceeds threshold. Relocate and retry if insufficient. Block
compression until confirmed. Do not compress on a floating anchor.

Stage 3 — Fill

Load mold to current fill_volume_target. Fill volume is the only
parameter adjusted by the calibration loop — geometry target does not
move.

Stage 4 — Compress

Drive actuator to target press depth. Force path runs through anchor and
Earth, not chassis. Record compression force and depth.

Stage 5 — Eject and Dwell

Release press. Transfer block to ejection cradle. Hold for
ejection_dwell_seconds. No handoff until dwell complete.

Stage 6 — Hand Off

Transfer block to Verification / Logistics with fabrication record.
Await Verification result.

Stage 7 — Calibration Update

Receive Verification feedback: density result, fill volume correction
delta, recommended dwell. Update fill_volume_target and
ejection_dwell_seconds for next cycle. Increment calibration cycle
counter if block was rejected.

Stage 8 — Convergence Check

If calibration_cycle_count exceeds convergence limit: stop pressing,
flag soil batch to House Brain with full calibration history. Wait for
instruction — do not continue on bad soil.

Stage 9 — Feed Quality Emit

After each batch, emit fabricator_feed_quality_record to Excavator and
House Brain: calibration cycles, fill volume delta, conditioning
applied, reject rate, source zone ID.

13. Calibration Loop Detail

The calibration loop is the core of Fabricator's intelligence. It is not
a sensor system. It is a feedback cycle.

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  The invariant rule: Target geometry never changes. Fill volume is the
  only knob. Assembly's dimensional requirement is sacred. Everything
  else bends to it.

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

Calibration States

- too_light → increase fill volume by delta

- in_spec → no change, reset calibration counter

- too_dense / overloaded → decrease fill volume by delta

- calibration_limit_exceeded → stop, flag, request new feed or
  intervention

Calibration Block Policy

- First 3–5 blocks of any new soil patch are expected calibration blocks

- Calibration blocks are recycled back into the feed hopper — not wasted

- Calibration cycle count resets on each new identified soil patch

- Calibration block count is reported to House Brain so pipeline
  scheduling can account for it

Convergence Signal to Excavator

- Batches that converge in 1–2 cycles: gate threshold is well-tuned

- Batches that need 5+ cycles: gate is too permissive for this zone

- Batches that hit convergence limit: flag zone as poor quality feed
  source

16. Failure Modes

Anchor-Side Failures

- Anchor fails to seat in loose or wet ground

- Anchor seating confidence below threshold after relocation attempts

- Anchor seizes mid-cycle — compression force transmitted to chassis

- Anchor retraction failure after compression

Feed and Conditioning Failures

- Incoming moisture probe failure — conditioning gate blind

- Moisture too extreme for two-condition gate to compensate

- Water reservoir empty — conditioning unavailable

- Feed hopper clog — fill volume inconsistent

Calibration Failures

- Calibration loop fails to converge — soil patch too variable

- Convergence limit hit — soil flagged, production stops

- Verification feedback delayed — fill volume target stale

- Fill volume correction overshoots — oscillation in calibration loop

Compression and Ejection Failures

- Compression force insufficient — actuator underperforms

- Block sticks in mold — ejection incomplete

- Block damaged in cradle — dwell too short

- Ejection cradle misalignment — block corners exposed to lateral force

System-Side Failures

- Wrong recipe loaded

- Stale Survey moisture data — conditioning mismatch

- Feed quality record not emitted — Excavator gate calibration gap

- Verification feedback channel broken — fill volume target frozen

17. Success Criteria

Fabricator is successful if it can:

12. Produce dimensionally consistent blocks from variable soil feed

13. Confirm anchor seating before every compression cycle — never
    compress on a floating anchor

14. Apply minimum conditioning without becoming a soil lab

15. Converge the calibration loop within the defined limit on normal
    soil

16. Correctly escalate soil batches that exceed convergence limit rather
    than looping indefinitely

17. Protect freshly-pressed blocks in the ejection cradle until handling
    strength is reached

18. Receive Verification feedback and update fill volume and dwell
    without human intervention

19. Emit feed quality records that allow the Excavator to tune its gate
    over time

18. Environment Assumptions

Fabricator assumes:

- Surface sorting container provides a coarse-qualified soil feed

- Ground is suitable for anchor deployment within the work zone — not
  solid bedrock, not fully saturated

- Verification is available at end of each production or calibration
  cycle

- House Brain provides recipe and convergence limit before production
  begins

- Survey has provided zone moisture data before the first feed batch
  arrives

- Excavator has an input channel to receive feed quality records

19. Build Phases

Phase 1 — Static Compression Bench

Build:

- Ground anchor system

- Fixed mold geometry

- Compression actuator

- Anchor seating confirmation via motor current

Goal: prove Earth Anvil principle — compress to spec without machine
mass.

Phase 2 — Fill Volume Calibration Loop

Add:

- Fill volume encoder

- Verification feedback receiver

- Dynamic fill volume update logic

- Calibration cycle counter

Goal: prove fill volume loop converges on variable test soil within N
cycles.

Phase 3 — Moisture Conditioning Gate

Add:

- Inline moisture probe

- Water conditioning reservoir

- Two-condition gate logic

- Survey moisture pre-warning consumer

Goal: prove Fabricator handles soil at the dry and wet extremes without
calibration loop failure.

Phase 4 — Ejection Cradle and Dwell

Add:

- Ejection support cradle

- Dwell timer

- Verification-tuned dwell feedback

Goal: prove blocks arrive at Verification without handling damage.

Phase 5 — Convergence Limit and Escalation

Add:

- Convergence limit enforcement

- Soil patch flagging to House Brain

- Calibration block recycling path

Goal: prove Fabricator cannot be trapped in an infinite calibration
spiral.

Phase 6 — Feed Quality Emit and House Brain Integration

Connect:

- Feed quality record channel to Excavator

- Full House Brain command and telemetry loop

- Zone navigation for multi-position press operation

Goal: make Fabricator a full participant in the system feedback loop —
not just a press.

20. Cost Direction

Fabricator stays cheap by:

- Using Earth mass instead of machine mass for compression force

- Using a ball screw or toggle joint instead of a multi-ton hydraulic
  frame

- Replacing an onboard soil lab with a two-condition moisture gate

- Replacing quality intelligence with a feedback tether to Verification

- Keeping geometry fixed and only tuning fill volume — one variable
  instead of many

- Using the Survey Robot's existing moisture data rather than new
  sensors

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  C2A Principle: Borrow the Earth's mass. Fix the shape. Tune the fill.
  Let Verification be the brain. Feed what you learned back to Excavator.

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

21. Out of Scope / Not This Robot's Job

Fabricator does not:

- Determine final recipe suitability or structural role

- Certify block quality — that is Verification's job

- Dig or classify soil — that is Excavator's job

- Transport finished blocks to the build site — that is Logistics

- Place blocks — that is Assembly

Those belong to: Excavator, Verification, Logistics, Assembly, and House
Brain.

Fabricator only says: "Given this earth feed and this recipe, I can
produce dimensionally consistent blocks and converge on the correct
density with Verification's guidance."

22. Final Blueprint Summary

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  The Fabricator Robot is a ground-anchored, earth-pressing machine that
    ingests the Excavator's coarse-qualified soil feed, applies minimum
  moisture conditioning via a two-condition gate, confirms anchor seating
   before every compression cycle, compresses to a fixed geometry using
      the Earth as the anvil, tunes fill volume dynamically through a
      Verification feedback loop bounded by a hard convergence limit,
  protects freshly-pressed blocks in an ejection cradle, and feeds batch
    quality data back to the Excavator to improve its gate calibration
                         across all future builds.
